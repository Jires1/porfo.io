Félix Moumié

The man who had growing activism

  • Félix-Roland Moumié was a Cameroonian anti-colonialist leader who was assassinated in Geneva on November 3, 1960, by an agent of the SDECE (French secret service) with thallium, following official independence from France earlier that year.
  • He was born on November 1, 1925, near Foumban in the Noun department of the West region of Cameroon.
  • Moumié was a physician and a politician who served as the Secretary-General of the Union des populations du Cameroun (UPC) from September 13, 1958, to November 3, 1960.
  • He succeeded Ruben Um Nyobè as the leader of the fight for Cameroon’s independence
  • Moumié was officially proclaimed a national hero by the law of December 16, 1991, of the National Assembly of Cameroon
